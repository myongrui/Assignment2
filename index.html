What exactly is Covid-19 and how does it spread?

Otherwise known as Coronavirus disease, is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 Virus. This causes most of the infected to experience mild to moderate respiratory illness and recover without requiring special treatment. Infected people will experience mild to moderate respiratory illness and can recover without any special treatment. The virus can spreads through the infected person's mouth or nose in small liquid particles.

Origin of Covid-19 Virus

Thought to be origined from animals, it is most likely a spillover infection (spread from one species to another) from bats to humans. Phylogenetics estimate that SARS-CoV-2 arose in October or November 2019, phylogenetic algorithm analysis have implied that it may have already been circulating in Guangdong before Wuhan, where the first human infection was confirmed.

Timeline of Covid-19

  1. 1 December 2019
    Earliest onset of symptoms reported
  2. 18 December 2019
    Traces of virus found in waste water samples in Milan and Turin, Italy
  3. 20 January 2020
    First human-to-human transmission reported
  4. 30 January 2020
    World Health Organization declared Public Health Emergency of International Concern
  5. 11 March 2020
    World Health Organization declared Covid-19 a pandemic
  6. 2 April 2020
    Confirmed cases of Covid-19 hit 1 million worldwide
